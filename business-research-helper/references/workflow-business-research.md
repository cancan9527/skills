# Workflow Business Research

## Purpose

This reference strengthens business research for workflow-heavy topics such as:

- purchasing
- incoming inspection
- warehousing
- approvals
- production handoffs
- quality processes
- cross-system writebacks

Use it when the topic is not just a feature idea, but a real business chain with multiple roles, states, exceptions, and downstream consequences.

## Core Principle

For workflow-heavy business research, do not stop at “what page is needed”.

You need to understand:

- who acts
- when they act
- what triggers the next step
- what can block the flow
- what data is written back
- who owns the result

## Reinforced Research Dimensions

### 1. Start and End Conditions

Ask:

- what counts as the real start of this process?
- what counts as true completion?
- is there an intermediate “recorded but not effective yet” state?

### 2. Role Handoffs

Ask:

- where does one role hand off to another?
- what information must be complete before handoff?
- what usually goes wrong at the handoff point?

### 3. State Changes

Ask:

- what statuses exist today, formally or informally?
- what event changes one status into another?
- are parent and child statuses both involved?
- which statuses are terminal and which can be reversed?

### 4. Decision Rules

Ask:

- what rules decide pass/fail, approval/rejection, release/block, or normal/exception?
- are there thresholds, tolerances, or conditional allowances?
- are there “allowed but must explain” cases?

### 5. Reverse and Exception Flows

Ask:

- when can someone withdraw or reject?
- who can trigger a rollback?
- what gets reset after rollback?
- what happens to linked records after rejection?

### 6. Data and Writebacks

Ask:

- which upstream record does this process consume?
- which downstream record does it create or update?
- after each major step, what field changes?
- which system owns the final truth?

### 7. Pain Points in Real Operations

Ask:

- where do people repeatedly argue about numbers, status, or ownership?
- where do users still keep side records in spreadsheets or chat?
- which exception cases cause the most rework?
- where do multiple systems drift apart?

## Typical Workflow-Heavy Topics

### Purchasing / Incoming

Focus on:

- arrival vs receipt vs usable inventory
- partial arrival
- over-receipt tolerance
- supplier batch information
- purchasing line writeback

### Incoming Inspection / Quality

Focus on:

- inspection record vs stock record
- pass / fail / concession handling
- quality status on inventory
- attachment or evidence requirements
- whether rejection creates abnormal closure work

### Approval Flows

Focus on:

- who really decides
- what can be returned
- what must be filled before submission
- whether approval changes business effectiveness or only records consent

### Warehousing

Focus on:

- inventory effect timing
- available vs blocked vs pending-review stock
- location or batch updates
- whether reversal reverts stock state

## Output Reminder

When this reference is used, make sure the final research output explicitly includes:

- current role chain
- current process chain
- key status or milestone points
- major exception points
- upstream/downstream linkage
- unresolved rule questions

If the research result cannot support later status, button, or writeback design, it is still incomplete.

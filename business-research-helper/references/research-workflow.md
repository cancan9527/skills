# Research Workflow

## Purpose

This reference defines the standard workflow for business research before or during requirement refinement.

Use it to:

- decide what kind of research the current task needs
- avoid jumping straight into solution design
- structure research findings so they can support PRD and requirement work later

## Core Principle

Business research is not the same as writing a solution.

The goal is to answer:

1. how the business works now
2. where the pain points and ambiguity are
3. which rules or decisions are still unclear
4. what information is still missing before requirement definition

## Standard Research Flow

Use this sequence by default:

1. define the research topic
2. identify the current trigger or problem
3. clarify the participating roles
4. map the current process
5. extract judgment rules
6. identify exceptions and edge cases
7. surface pain points and risks
8. summarize requirement opportunities and remaining gaps

## Scenario Selection

### 1. Research Preparation

Use when the user is about to talk to business users and needs a good interview structure.

Main output:

- interview agenda
- key question list
- role-based question packs

### 2. Mid-Requirement Research

Use when a PRD or requirement is already half written but important logic is still unclear.

Main output:

- missing business questions
- unclear rule list
- requirement补调研 checklist

### 3. Post-Interview Organization

Use when the user already has notes and needs structured整理.

Main output:

- business current state summary
- role and process summary
- key pain points
- remaining questions

## What Good Research Should Capture

At minimum, the research output should capture:

- business objective
- current actors
- current steps
- decision points
- exceptions
- data ownership
- pain points
- next-step requirement gaps

If one of these is missing, the later requirement phase is likely to become unstable.

## Transition to Requirement Work

After business research, the result should be able to support one or more of these next steps:

- refining the requirement scope
- writing a PRD
- reviewing an existing PRD
- drawing a workflow
- defining status and button rules

Do not end research with only raw notes. Always turn it into a structured summary that the next artifact can inherit.
